Biodegradable Polyester / Cristalline Nanocellulose Nanocomposites for Biomedical Applications: Preparation and Characterization.

Amaia Butron, Olatz Llorente, Jorge Fernandez, Emilio Meaurio, Joserra Sarasua
EHU

Abstract

The use of biodegradable polymers in biomedicine is rising, and composites loaded with reinforcements are an ideal choice for implants, bone cements and drug delivery systems. The matrix of poly(ethylene brassylate) was reinforced with cellulose nanocrystals, in order to
achieve
a
material
with superior mechanical properties and higher thermal stability. The composite was characterized through calorimetry, tensile tests and thermogravimetry in order to determine the effect of different reinforcement loads. The Young modulus was found to increase about 20 % with just an addition of 2.5 wt% nanocellulose; and further addition of reinforcement resulted in the decrease of ductility of the composite.
