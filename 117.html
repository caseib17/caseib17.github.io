Right ventricular strain from cardiac cine MRI: a flattening approach

Gabriel Bernardino1, Bruno Paun1, Alvaro Sepulveda2, Merida Rodriguez2, Fatima Crispi2, Alexandra Groth3, Juergen Weese3, Miguel Angel Gonzalez-Ballester4, Constantine Butakoff1, Mathieu De Craene3, Bart Bijnens4
1Universitat Pompeu Fabra, 2Hospital Clinic, 3Philips, 4Universitat Pompeu Fabra, ICREA

Abstract

The right ventricle (RV) is responsible for the pulmonary circulation but few image-based indices focus on the RV shape or deformation. This omission is likely due to the complex RV morphology with respect to the left ventricle (LV). Therefore, it is relevant to find new methods able to quantify RV shape and function. In this abstract, we present a novel automatic method to analyze RV deformation from cine cardiac magnetic resonance images (cMRI). We segmented and registered the cMRI to obtain a 3D mesh of the RV for different instants of the cardiac cycle.

To study the deformation in both longitudinal and circumferential directions we used a flattening method to generate a grid on the RV surface. As the grid deforms with the RV dynamics, we measured shortenings and lengthenings to compute the engineering strain. This method allows to obtain regional strains.

We compared computed strain traces with strain acquired using Speckle tracking in 2D echocardiography, obtaining similar results.
