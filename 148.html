Multiscale Entropy and Multiscale Time Irreversibility for Atrial Fibrillation and Heart Failure from 7-Day Holter

Mohammed Elyaagoubi1, Rebeca Goya-Esteban2, Younes Jabrane3, Sergio Moñoz Romero2, Jose Luis Rojo-Alvarez2, Arcadi García Arberola4
1Rey Juan Carlos University, 2Universidad Rey Juan Carlos, 3Cadi Ayyad University, 4Hospital Clínico Universitario Virgen de la Arrixaca de Murcia

Abstract

To prevent the sudden death risk, several methods have been proposed in literature based on Heart Rate Variability (HRV) which is related with the state of the autonomic nervous system. To quantify the HRV, the measurement of the irregularity based on Entropy methods Multiscale Entropy and Multiscale time irreversibility was performed. Higher entropy values have been reported in the literature for healthy states than for some pathologies, with the hypothesis that decreasing entropy points to perturbations of the complex physiological mechanisms . To date, the MSE and MTI have been analyzed for monitoring of a maximum of 24 hours, and a limit of 20 scales. In this work, we first analyze entropy and time irreversibility on known synthetic signals. MSE and MTI methods are first used to analyze these synthetic signals, by increasing the number of scales. Then, MSE and MTI are applied to 7-days monitoring analysis of two data sets, namely, 3 patients with Atrial Fibrillation and 3 patients with Heart Failure, by increasing the number of scales up to 100, thanks to the long-term duration of the holter recordings. Our results show that the elevated scales contain relevant information about the patient's condition, which changes according to the pathology and the patient, therefore, it paves the way to scfutinize these techniques in more detail for their use in long-term cardiac monitoring scenarios.
